My Datastory

This is the three-stage process of how I found my life to be intertwined with data, writing, and digital media. I explored how music is an example of how something so seemingly unquantifiable can display a significant trend and further add to how the internet can infringe on my privacy.

Data Collection

To begin to uncover the connection between data and my life, I chose to focus on something that has always been, and will always be, an integral part of my life: Music. I noticed that throughout my day, I listen to all kinds of music. However, my music is constantly interrupted due to scheduled activity.

I chose to record what song type I listened to and how I felt at that time on a scale of 1-10. I only chose to record the first song of every session because it is chosen and meaningful rather than a shuffle of my playlist. I then recorded several other factors such as the time of day, temperature, and stress level. This table is the result of consistently recording my music and corresponding mood for a week.

I chose something that was routine and therefore easier to record. I made sure to add as much context as I could to my data, but also found most of it to be useless because of the lack of a trend. That's ok. Even so, it showed that a lot of what goes unnoticed could be considered as data and possibly used.

Data Visualization

This is a visualization is what I saw to be most stark in the data I collected over the week. I seperated the data into different reoccuring feelings over the week. Each emotion firework has an explosion that relates explosion size and level of stress. Each explosion has a colored fragments that are instances of a genre played.

I learned there was a significant trend between being happy, listening to Hip-Hop, and having the least amount of stress. There is correlation even though it may not be incredibly apparent due to the sample size. You could argue that Hip-Hop can invoke and connotes a lighter mood.

Perhaps it could then be used to advertise something to me. Perhaps, with Hip-Hop playing in the background, I would be more inclined to buy the service. The point being that my preferences and media use can be quantified and used in some way.

Explained Video

This video is how my own data can be a commodity to Facebook. I chose to specifically explore data brokering and how the music streaming service I use can be used by Facebook in an effort to create a better experience.

Facebook's Connect with Facebook service is how my data can become a part of the digitalization of me. Facebook has access to everything I do on their website. With the addition of their service, they can add different perspectives. This is because all partners of Facebook who provide the account service will receive Facebook's data on you in exchange for their own data on you. In the case of music, since I found that there was a trend within my use, my music choice could potentially add to Facebook's algorithms of advertisement towards me.

Using my music preference is a stretch, but also indicates that this idea can be applied to seemingly arbitrary things. If my music can be quantified, think of how my digital media use could illustrate who I am from different perspectives. Unless I choose to cut out digital media from my life, the collection of what I consider private is inevitable.
