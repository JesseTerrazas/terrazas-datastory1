My Datastory

This is the three-stage process of how I found my life to be intertwined with data, writing, and digital media. I explored how music is an example of how something so seemingly unquantifiable can display a significant trend and further add to how the internet can infringe on my privacy.

Data Collection

To begin to uncover the connection between data and I, I chose to focus on something that has always been, and will always be, an integral part of my life: Music. I noticed that throughout my day, I listen to all kinds of music. However, my music is constantly interrupted due to scheduled activity.

I chose to record what song type I listened to and how I felt at that time on a scale of 1-10. I only chose to record the first song of every session because it is chosen and meaningful rather than a shuffle of my playlist. I then added several other factors such as the time of day, temperature, and stress level. This table is the result of consistently recording my music and corresponding mood for a week.

Data Visualization

This is a visualization is what I saw to be most stark in the data I collected over the week. I seperated the data into different reoccuring feelings over the week. Each emotion firework has an explosion that relates explosion size and level of stress. Each explosion has a colored fragment that is an instance of that genre played.

I learned there was a significant trend between being happy, listening to Hip-Hop, and having the least amount of stress. The point is that there is correlation even though it may not be incredibly apparent due to the sample size. You could argue that Hip-Hop can invoke and connotes a lighter mood. Perhaps it could then be used to advertise something to me.

Explained Video

This video is how my own data can be a commodity to influencial companies. I chose to specifically explore data brokering and how the music streaming service I use can be used by Facebook in an effort to create a better experience.

Facebook's Connect with Facebook service is how my data can become a part of the digitalization of me. Facebook has access to everything I do on their website and with their service they can add different perspectives. In the case of music, since I found that there was a trend within my use, my music choice could potentially add to Facebook's algorithms.
